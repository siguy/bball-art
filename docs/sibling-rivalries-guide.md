# Sibling Rivalry Cards - Planning Guide

This document captures potential sibling rivalry pairings for Torah Titans, with template recommendations, scene suggestions, and biblical verses for each.

---

## Template Overview

We have three sibling rivalry templates:

| Template | Style | Best For |
|----------|-------|----------|
| **sibling-duality** | Yin-yang with gold ring, embossed scripture | Interconnected opposites, cosmic rivalries, philosophical depth |
| **sibling-card** | Double-headed reversible playing card | Equals in opposition, neither above the other |
| **sibling-icon** | Byzantine diptych with gold leaf | Divine judgment, righteous vs wicked, sacred art |

### Key Architecture

**Template defines STRUCTURE:**
- Visual layout, composition rules
- Where scripture goes, how the ring looks
- The concept of "seed dots" (each sibling contains a piece of the other)

**Pairing data defines CONTENT:**
- Specific scripture for the ring
- Specific imagery for each side
- Colors, traits, seed symbols
- For triptychs: acts with progression

## Additional Proposed Pairings

### 1. Cain & Abel

**The Story:** The first brothers, the first murder. Cain the farmer, Abel the shepherd. God accepted Abel's offering but rejected Cain's. Cain killed Abel in the field.

**Recommended Template:** `sibling-icon` (Byzantine diptych)
- This story is about divine judgment - God favoring one offering over another
- The halo system works perfectly: Abel gets the halo, Cain does not
- Byzantine gravitas fits the weight of the first murder

**Key Scenes:**

| Scene | Cain | Abel | Visual |
|-------|------|------|--------|
| **The Offerings** | Offering grain/fruit, smoke descends/rejected | Offering firstborn lamb, smoke rises/accepted | Split composition: smoke going opposite directions |
| **The Murder** | Raising rock/weapon, face of rage | Fallen or defending, innocent | The moment before or after |
| **The Mark** | Marked by God, wandering | Absent (blood crying from ground) | Solo Cain card possible |

**Scripture Options:**

| Verse | Hebrew | English | Best For |
|-------|--------|---------|----------|
| Genesis 4:4-5 | וַיִּשַׁע יְהוָה אֶל־הֶבֶל וְאֶל־מִנְחָתוֹ וְאֶל־קַיִן וְאֶל־מִנְחָתוֹ לֹא שָׁעָה | "The Lord looked with favor on Abel and his offering, but on Cain and his offering He did not look" | The offerings scene |
| Genesis 4:9 | הֲשֹׁמֵר אָחִי אָנֹכִי | "Am I my brother's keeper?" | Cain's defiance |
| Genesis 4:10 | קוֹל דְּמֵי אָחִיךָ צֹעֲקִים אֵלַי מִן־הָאֲדָמָה | "The voice of your brother's blood cries out to Me from the ground" | The aftermath |

**Duality Concept:**
- Cain: Earth tones, grain, descending smoke, field
- Abel: Pastoral whites/creams, lamb, ascending smoke, shepherd's crook
- Seed symbols: Grain sheaf in Abel's domain, lamb in Cain's domain

**Righteous Figure:** Abel (gets halo in sibling-icon)

### 2. Isaac & Ishmael

**The Story:** Abraham's two sons - Ishmael by Hagar the handmaid, Isaac by Sarah the wife. Ishmael was the firstborn but Isaac was the promised heir. Hagar and Ishmael were cast out into the wilderness.

**Recommended Template:** `sibling-duality` (yin-yang)
- Not a clear "righteous vs wicked" - both became great nations
- The duality of legitimate heir vs firstborn
- Both are blessed, just differently

**Key Scenes:**

| Scene | Isaac | Ishmael | Visual |
|-------|-------|---------|--------|
| **The Banishment** | Young boy at Sarah's side, in the tent | Young boy with Hagar, walking into wilderness | Tent vs desert |
| **The Wells** | Beer-lahai-roi, wells of life | Wilderness well where angel found Hagar | Water imagery |
| **The Burial** | Together burying Abraham | Together burying Abraham | Reconciliation moment |

**Scripture Options:**

| Verse | Hebrew | English | Best For |
|-------|--------|---------|----------|
| Genesis 21:10 | כִּי לֹא יִירַשׁ בֶּן־הָאָמָה הַזֹּאת עִם־בְּנִי עִם־יִצְחָק | "The son of this handmaid shall not inherit with my son, with Isaac" | The conflict |
| Genesis 21:13 | וְגַם אֶת־בֶּן־הָאָמָה לְגוֹי אֲשִׂימֶנּוּ כִּי זַרְעֲךָ הוּא | "Also the son of the handmaid I will make into a nation, for he is your seed" | Both blessed |
| Genesis 25:9 | וַיִּקְבְּרוּ אֹתוֹ יִצְחָק וְיִשְׁמָעֵאל בָּנָיו | "His sons Isaac and Ishmael buried him" | Reconciliation |

**Duality Concept:**
- Isaac: Soft golds, tent dweller, wells, promised laughter
- Ishmael: Desert browns/reds, wilderness archer, wild donkey imagery
- Seed symbols: Tent in Ishmael's domain, bow in Isaac's domain

### 3. Joseph & His Brothers

**The Story:** Jacob's favorite son, hated by his brothers for his dreams and his coat. Sold into slavery, rose to power in Egypt, eventually reconciled with his brothers.

**Recommended Template:** `sibling-card` (double-headed)
- Many brothers, but can represent as "Joseph vs The Brothers"
- The reversal: sold as slave, becomes ruler they bow to
- Rotational symmetry captures the reversal of fortune

**Alternative:** `sibling-duality` for Joseph vs Judah specifically (Judah suggested selling him, later offered himself as hostage for Benjamin)

**Key Scenes:**

| Scene | Joseph | Brothers | Visual |
|-------|--------|----------|--------|
| **The Coat** | Wearing coat of many colors, dreaming | Stripping the coat, faces of jealousy | The coat as central symbol |
| **The Pit** | In the pit, looking up | Standing above, deciding his fate | Vertical composition |
| **The Revelation** | As Egyptian vizier, powerful | Bowing before him, not recognizing | Power reversal |
| **Reconciliation** | Weeping, embracing Benjamin | Weeping, guilt transformed to relief | Emotional reunion |

**Scripture Options:**

| Verse | Hebrew | English | Best For |
|-------|--------|---------|----------|
| Genesis 37:4 | וַיִּשְׂנְאוּ אֹתוֹ וְלֹא יָכְלוּ דַּבְּרוֹ לְשָׁלֹם | "They hated him and could not speak peaceably to him" | The rivalry |
| Genesis 37:19 | הִנֵּה בַּעַל הַחֲלֹמוֹת הַלָּזֶה בָּא | "Behold, this dreamer is coming" | The mockery |
| Genesis 45:4 | אֲנִי יוֹסֵף אֲחִיכֶם אֲשֶׁר־מְכַרְתֶּם אֹתִי מִצְרָיְמָה | "I am Joseph your brother, whom you sold into Egypt" | The revelation |
| Genesis 50:20 | אַתֶּם חֲשַׁבְתֶּם עָלַי רָעָה אֱלֹהִים חֲשָׁבָהּ לְטֹבָה | "You intended evil against me, but God intended it for good" | Reconciliation |

**Duality Concept:**
- Joseph: Royal Egyptian garb OR the multicolored coat, dreams, sheaves bowing
- Brothers: Shepherds' clothing, the pit, guilt and jealousy
- The coat could be the central divider element

### 4. Moses & Miriam (& Aaron)

**The Story:** The three siblings who led Israel out of Egypt. Miriam saved baby Moses, led the women in song at the sea, but later challenged Moses' authority and was struck with leprosy.

**Recommended Template:** `sibling-icon` (Byzantine diptych)
- There's a clear moment of divine judgment (Miriam's leprosy)
- The reconciliation (Moses prays for her healing)
- Could also work as a three-way icon (triptych with Aaron)

**Key Scenes:**

| Scene | Moses | Miriam | Visual |
|-------|-------|--------|--------|
| **The Basket** | Baby in basket | Young girl watching over him | The Nile, reeds, protective love |
| **The Song** | Staff raised, sea parted | Timbrel raised, leading women in dance | Celebration, parallel leadership |
| **The Challenge** | Humble, not defending himself | Speaking against Moses' Cushite wife | Tension, divine cloud descending |
| **The Healing** | Praying for Miriam | Struck white with leprosy, outside camp | Moses' intercession |

**Scripture Options:**

| Verse | Hebrew | English | Best For |
|-------|--------|---------|----------|
| Exodus 15:20 | וַתִּקַּח מִרְיָם הַנְּבִיאָה אֲחוֹת אַהֲרֹן אֶת־הַתֹּף בְּיָדָהּ | "Miriam the prophetess, the sister of Aaron, took a timbrel in her hand" | The song/celebration |
| Numbers 12:1 | וַתְּדַבֵּר מִרְיָם וְאַהֲרֹן בְּמֹשֶׁה | "Miriam and Aaron spoke against Moses" | The challenge |
| Numbers 12:13 | אֵל נָא רְפָא נָא לָהּ | "Please God, heal her please" | Moses' prayer |
| Micah 6:4 | וָאֶשְׁלַח לְפָנֶיךָ אֶת־מֹשֶׁה אַהֲרֹן וּמִרְיָם | "I sent before you Moses, Aaron, and Miriam" | Their shared leadership |

**Duality Concept:**
- Moses: Staff, tablets, veil over face, humble posture
- Miriam: Timbrel, water (Well of Miriam), prophetess garb
- The well could be central - it followed Israel for Miriam's merit

### 5. Ephraim & Manasseh

**The Story:** Joseph's two sons, blessed by their grandfather Jacob. Jacob deliberately crossed his hands to give the greater blessing to Ephraim (the younger) over Manasseh (the firstborn).

**Recommended Template:** `sibling-duality` (yin-yang)
- The crossed hands blessing is visually powerful
- The younger over the elder theme
- Less dramatic rivalry, more about divine choice

**Key Scene:**

The Crossed Hands Blessing - Jacob on his deathbed, hands crossed, blessing the boys. Ephraim at his right hand (but Jacob's hands are crossed so right hand is on younger). Joseph trying to correct his father.

**Scripture Options:**

| Verse | Hebrew | English | Best For |
|-------|--------|---------|----------|
| Genesis 48:14 | וַיְשַׁכֵּל אֶת־יָדָיו כִּי מְנַשֶּׁה הַבְּכוֹר | "He crossed his hands, for Manasseh was the firstborn" | The blessing moment |
| Genesis 48:19 | גַּם־הוּא יִהְיֶה־לְּעָם וְגַם־הוּא יִגְדָּל וְאוּלָם אָחִיו הַקָּטֹן יִגְדַּל מִמֶּנּוּ | "He also shall become a people, and he also shall be great; but his younger brother shall be greater" | The prophecy |
| Genesis 48:20 | יְשִׂמְךָ אֱלֹהִים כְּאֶפְרַיִם וְכִמְנַשֶּׁה | "God make you like Ephraim and Manasseh" | The blessing that became tradition |

**Duality Concept:**
- Both boys similar in appearance (brothers, not opposites)
- The visual focus is Jacob's crossed hands
- Could show the blessing as flowing from crossed hands
- Less contrast than other rivalries - more subtle

## Implementation Checklist

For each pairing, create:

1. **Pairing JSON file:** `data/series/torah-titans/pairings/{pairing-id}.json`
   - Include `seriesSpecificData.siblingDuality` (or siblingCard/siblingIcon)
   - Scripture with Hebrew (including vowels), English, source
   - Colors, imagery, traits for each character
   - Seed symbols
   - For triptych: acts with progression

2. **Pose files:** `data/poses/figures/{character}.json`
   - Default pose
   - Rivalry-specific poses
   - Scene-specific poses if doing triptych

3. **Generate test cards:**
   ```bash
   node scripts/generate-card.js {pairing-id} {template} --series torah-titans
   ```

4. **For triptych progression:**
   ```bash
   node scripts/generate-card.js {pairing-id} sibling-duality --series torah-titans --act 1
   node scripts/generate-card.js {pairing-id} sibling-duality --series torah-titans --act 2
   node scripts/generate-card.js {pairing-id} sibling-duality --series torah-titans --act 3
   ```
